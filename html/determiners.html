Determiners

Determiners are words which come at the beginning of noun phrases.There are specific determiners and general determiners.

Specific determiners

The specific determiners are:

  • possessives: my, your, his, her, its, our, their, whose
  • demonstratives: this, that, these, those

A specific determiner is used when the reader knows exactly what we are referring to.

  • Thank you very much for your letter.
  • Look at those lovely flowers.
  • Whose coat is this?

General determiners

A general determiner is used when talking about things in general and the reader does not know exactly what we are reffering to.

The general determiners are:

  • 0(no determiner)

    • no determiner is used with plural nouns and uncount nouns.

    • Girls normally do better in school than boys.
    • Milk is very good for you.
  • any

    • The general determiner any is used with a singular noun or an uncount noun when talking about all of those people or things.

    • Any child can do it.
    • He can drives any vehicle.
  • another

    • The general determiner another is used when talking about an additional person or thing.

    • I have another option for a job.
    • He wants a another wine glass.
  • other

    • The plural form is another is other.

    • John play football with Peter and his other friends.
    • He start a new project with other companies.
